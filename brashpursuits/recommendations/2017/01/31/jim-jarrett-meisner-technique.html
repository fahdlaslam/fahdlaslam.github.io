Jim Jarrett & The Meisner Technique Studio

If you’re reading this, odds are you’ve encountered the Meisner Technique in one form or another. A lot of great actors have put it to incredible use, and Meisner himself is, it goes without saying, an absolute legend in the world of acting. 

My own personal journey with the Meisner Technique is a varied and problematic one. I’ve studied under multiple teachers, a few good, a few bad, and one or two incredible enough to actually deserve the title. I find Meisner to be quite possibly the most perfect acting teacher ever available to us, the only problem being, he’s not around anymore. And a lot of his students, for better or worse, loved him enough to vehemently claim his name for their own, regardless of how qualified they are to do so. There’s a lot of in-fighting about what the technique is and what it isn’t, and many a teacher will tell you that “their version” of Meisner is “the truth.” To be frank, I’ve encountered more ego in my Meisner classes than I’ve encountered pretty much anywhere else. 

Today though, I’d like to share with you a Meisner teacher who’s doing things right.

Now, I can’t speak to Jim Jarrett’s flesh and blood, brick and mortar class room experience. He teaches out of San Francisco. I live in New York. We’ve never had a conversation. But, he has managed to do something that has caught my attention.

He has decided to give away part of himself.

Charting a course towards becoming an actor is one of the most daringly personal journeys a person can make. You’re offering yourself up to the eyes of other people. Sure, you might end up cashing million dollar paychecks to do it, but you also might not.

So it means something to me when an acting teacher is willing to minister to the disciples free of charge. The smallest advice, given at no personal gain for the giver, is a form of currency we don’t have enough of in the acting world. Everybody seems to want to keep the path a secret. Probably out of fear that you’ll realize they don’t actually have any idea where it was to begin with.

Jim Jarrett offers a free newsletter and scores of engaging videos which are an invaluable resource for actors at any level. Time and thought are put into these missives, and they’re both inspiring and educational. Trust me, I don’t go in for spam-based bullshit meant to sell me something. Ever. Jim is cutting through all that and using the internet for its purest function: delivering worthwhile thoughts and learning points to people who can use them.

So go forth, and use them eagerly.

You can check out more about Jim Jarrett, his newsletter, videos, and classes at www.themeisnertechniquestudio.com. If you have any questions, comments, or inquiries about this article, please shoot an email to tim@brashpursuits.com. Have a great day!
