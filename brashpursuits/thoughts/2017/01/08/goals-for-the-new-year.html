Goals for the New Year

I hate resolutions. They’re cheesy, and as far as I can tell, they never work. But, in the interest of taking my career more seriously, the start of a new year seems like as good a time as any to establish what my goals for the immediate future are. So, here’s a list of my acting goals in 2016.

  1. Get An Agent. I admittedly don’t have much of a concrete plan for doing this, but I’ve just about reached the point in my career where it’s necessary.
  2. Build A Polished Reel. I have several isolated videos that give a good idea how I look and work on camera, but I’ve yet to condense them down into a polished and digestible reel. This is quite possibly the easiest of my goals for the year, so it’s one I should work on immediately.
  3. Land Another Feature. Shooting my first feature film was the most valuable experience of my entire career. It gave me the clearest picture of all the challenges there are in performance, and finishing it was the most accomplished I’ve ever felt as an actor. I was exhausted afterwards, but with a few months away from it, I’m anxious to do more longform work.
  4. Write & Collaborate More. Besides being in front of camera or on stage, the writing I’ve done has most informed me as an actor. It really helps me figure out what kind of roles I want to play, what kind of projects I’m really passionate about, and what kind of work I want to do with others. It’s important to me in 2016 to write more, and to share what I write with others, with the honest intent to actually create it, instead of just leaving it in hyperspace.

I’ll admit. This list is a little more modest than I’d imagined it would be going in. I frequently daydream of myself accepting an Oscar or shooting a film with Tom Hardy. But, I’m semi-proud of myself for being able to actually create a list of actionable things I can actually commit to doing this year.

So, I’d like you to do the same. 

Create your own list of goals. Steal mine if you like them. Just give yourself a solid metric to work towards for the rest of the year, regardless of how simple or complex it might be. If it helps you to share your list with a friend, you can email it to me at tim@brashpursuits.com.

Let’s start taking things a little more seriously this year. Yeah?
